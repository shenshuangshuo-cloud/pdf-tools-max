How to Merge PDF Files on iPhone and iPad

Published: 2026-06-18 · PDF Tips

Introduction

The iPhone and iPad have quietly become powerful productivity tools, and with iOS 18, Apple has made PDF handling more capable than ever. Gone are the days when you needed a laptop to combine documents — now you can merge PDFs directly on your phone while commuting, in a meeting, or at a client's office. iOS 18 introduced enhanced PDF features in the Files app and improved Shortcuts automation, making it possible to combine documents without downloading any third-party app. This is particularly valuable for professionals who receive contracts, invoices, or reports as separate PDFs and need to send a single consolidated file. Whether you prefer using the built-in Files app, creating an automated Shortcut, or using a browser-based tool, this guide covers every reliable method for merging PDFs on iPhone and iPad in 2026. No app purchase, no subscription, no complicated setup required.

Method 1: Using the Files App (Built-in, iOS 18)

iOS 18's Files app now supports basic PDF page management. To merge PDFs, open the Files app and navigate to the folder containing your PDFs. Tap 'Select' in the top-right corner and choose the PDF files you want to combine. Tap the three-dot menu (More) at the bottom-right and look for the 'Create PDF' option — this combines the selected files into a single PDF in the order you selected them. Alternatively, you can open one PDF in the Files app's preview, tap the page thumbnail view, and drag another PDF file into the page list to append its pages. The resulting file is saved automatically to your Files. This method is completely free, requires no internet connection, and keeps your documents private on your device. The main limitation is that you cannot easily reorder pages between the two source files after combining, so select them in the correct order initially.

Method 2: Use PDF Craft (Free & No Upload Required)

  1. Open PDF Craft Merge PDF in Safari or Chrome on your iPhone
  2. Upload your PDF files — tap to select from Files, Photos, or iCloud Drive
  3. Arrange the files by dragging thumbnails into your preferred order
  4. Download the merged PDF back to your device

Why PDF Craft? Works perfectly on iPhone and iPad Safari. No app download needed, no file upload to servers — everything processes in your browser. Ideal when the Files app method is too limited.

Additional Tips & Best Practices

Regardless of which method you choose, there are a few universal best practices that will make your PDF workflow smoother. First, always keep a backup of your original files before merging or processing. While most tools are reliable, having a safety net gives you peace of mind. Second, name your files clearly before combining them — this saves time when you need to locate specific documents later. Third, if you are working with scanned documents, consider running OCR (Optical Character Recognition) first to ensure the text remains searchable after merging. Fourth, be mindful of file size limits if you plan to email the merged PDF — some email providers cap attachments at 25MB. Finally, test the merged PDF by opening it on at least two different devices or PDF viewers to ensure compatibility across platforms.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to download an app to merge PDFs on iPhone?

No. The built-in Files app in iOS 18 can combine PDFs via the 'Create PDF' option. For more control over page order, a browser-based tool like PDF Craft works without any app installation.

Can I merge PDFs on iPad the same way?

Yes. All methods described here work identically on iPad. The larger screen makes dragging thumbnails to reorder pages even easier.

Will merging reduce PDF quality on mobile?

No. Merging is a lossless operation — the text, images, and formatting remain identical to the source files regardless of whether you merge on a phone, tablet, or computer.

How do I merge a PDF with photos on iPhone?

First convert the photos to PDF (use the Print > Save as PDF trick in the Share sheet), then merge the resulting PDF with your other documents using the Files app or PDF Craft.
