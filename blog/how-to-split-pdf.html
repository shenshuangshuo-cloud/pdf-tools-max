How to Split PDF Files — Extract, Delete & Reorder Pages

Published: 2026-06-25 · PDF Tips

Introduction

Large PDF documents can be unwieldy. A 500-page report is hard to email, slow to scroll through, and frustrating to navigate. Splitting PDFs into smaller, more manageable files solves all of these problems. Beyond simple splitting, modern tools let you extract specific pages, delete unnecessary ones, and reorder content with precision. Use cases abound: extracting a single invoice page from a multi-page bank statement, splitting a combined presentation into individual slide decks, removing blank or filler pages, or isolating specific chapters of an e-book for easier reading. This guide covers the most efficient ways to split PDFs across all platforms, from simple page extraction to more advanced splitting patterns. Whether you need to split by page ranges, extract every Nth page, or break a document into equal parts, you will find the method that fits your workflow.

Method 1: Splitting by Page Range (Selective Extraction)

The most common splitting pattern is extracting a specific page range — say, pages 5 through 20 — from a larger document. To do this, open the PDF in a splitting tool and select the page range option. Enter the starting and ending page numbers, and the tool generates a new PDF containing only those pages. This is ideal for extracting a single chapter from an e-book, pulling contract pages for signature, or isolating relevant sections of a research paper. More advanced tools allow you to define multiple ranges in one operation — for example, extracting pages 1-10 and 25-35 as two separate files. Pay attention to page numbering: some tools use the PDF's internal page numbers, which may differ from the printed numbers if the document has a cover page or roman numeral preface. Always cross-reference with a page preview to avoid off-by-one errors.

Additional Tips & Best Practices

Regardless of which method you choose, a few universal best practices will improve your results. Always work on a copy of your original file rather than the original itself — this gives you a fallback if something goes wrong. If you are working with multi-page documents, process a single test page first to verify the output quality before running the full batch. Pay attention to the download format and ensure it matches your intended use case. Finally, if you are sharing the output with others, briefly test it on a different device or PDF viewer to confirm compatibility across platforms and avoid surprises.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I split a PDF into individual pages automatically?

Yes. Most splitting tools, including PDF Craft, have an 'extract all pages' mode that generates a separate PDF for each page. The files are typically delivered as a ZIP archive.

What happens to hyperlinks when I split a PDF?

Internal hyperlinks (links to other pages within the same PDF) may break if the target page is in a different split. External hyperlinks (links to websites) are preserved and continue working.

Can I split a PDF on my phone?

Yes. Browser-based splitting tools work on mobile devices. Upload from your phone's storage, select pages, and download the split files.

Is there a limit to how many pages I can split?

Browser-based tools are limited by your device's memory. PDF Craft handles documents up to several hundred pages smoothly. For very large documents, splitting in batches is recommended.
