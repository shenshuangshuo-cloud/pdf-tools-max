This addresses the primary privacy concern that kept many users tied to desktop software. Additional Tips & Best Practices

Regardless of which method you choose, there are a few universal best practices that will make your PDF workflow smoother. First, always keep a backup of your original files before merging or processing. While most tools are reliable, having a safety net gives you peace of mind. Second, name your files clearly before combining them — this saves time when you need to locate specific documents later. Third, if you are working with scanned documents, consider running OCR (Optical Character Recognition) first to ensure the text remains searchable after merging. Fourth, be mindful of file size limits if you plan to email the merged PDF — some email providers cap attachments at 25MB. Finally, test the merged PDF by opening it on at least two different devices or PDF viewers to ensure compatibility across platforms.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are free PDF tools as reliable as Adobe Acrobat?

For merging specifically, yes. Merging is a straightforward operation — combining page streams without altering content. Free tools handle this just as effectively as Adobe, often with a simpler interface.

Do free tools leave watermarks on merged PDFs?

Some do, but many do not. PDF Craft never adds watermarks. Always check the output of a new tool with a test file before relying on it for important documents.

Can I merge scanned PDFs with free tools?

Yes. Merging combines files regardless of whether they contain scanned images or digital text. However, if you need OCR (making scanned text searchable), you may need a separate OCR tool before merging.

What about LibreOffice or open-source alternatives?

LibreOffice Draw can open and arrange PDF pages, but its PDF editing capabilities are limited and may alter formatting. For simple merging, free online tools or Python libraries (PyPDF2, pdf-lib) are more reliable.
